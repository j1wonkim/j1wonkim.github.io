I study how international and domestic politics shape human rights and democratic norms (and vice versa). I use natural language processing, online survey experiments, and statistical modeling to address these questions. My work has appeared or is forthcoming in *Global Studies Quarterly*.

I am currently open to research and data positions for social good beginning in or after May 2026, with a preference for Atlanta-based/remote opportunities. Previously, I was a Visiting Assistant Professor in the Department of Data and Decision Sciences and a Research Faculty Fellow at the [Center of AI Learning](https://ailearning.emory.edu/) at Emory University.

I received my Ph.D. and M.A. in Political Science from Emory University, and B.A. in English literature and linguistics from Sungkyunkwan University.
