I am a data scientist and analyst focused on social and behavioral data, with a Ph.D. in Political Science and expertise in natural language processing, survey experiments, and statistical modeling. My academic work on how international and domestic politics shape human rights and democratic norms has appeared or is forthcoming in *Global Studies Quarterly*.

I am currently open to data and research roles for social impact beginning in or after May 2026, with a preference for Atlanta-based or remote opportunities. Previously, I was a Visiting Assistant Professor in the Department of Data and Decision Sciences and a Research Faculty Fellow at the [Center of AI Learning](https://ailearning.emory.edu/) at Emory University, where I partnered with NGOs to design research, inform strategic decision-making, and strengthen their data systems.

I hold a Ph.D. and M.A. in Political Science from Emory University, and a B.A. in English literature and linguistics from Sungkyunkwan University.
