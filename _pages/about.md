I study how international and domestic politics shape human rights and democratic norms (and vice versa). I use natural language processing, online survey experiments, and statistical modeling to address these questions. My work has appeared or is forthcoming in *Global Studies Quarterly*.

I am currently open to research and policy positions beginning in or after May 2026, with a preference for metro Atlanta-based, remote, and hybrid opportunities. Formerly, I was a Visiting Assistant Professor in the Department of Data and Decision Sciences and Research Faculty Fellow at [the Center of AI Learning](https://ailearning.emory.edu/) at Emory University.

I will receive my Ph.D. in Political Science from Emory University in May 2026. I have a B.A. in English literature and linguistics from Sungkyunkwan University.
